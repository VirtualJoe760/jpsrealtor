## Page Type 1: Medical Professional Home Loan Landing Page

### 1.1 Google Ads Strategy

#### Campaign Type Recommendation: Search (Primary) + Performance Max (Secondary)

**Search campaigns should be the primary driver** for physician mortgage leads. The reasoning:
- Users searching "physician mortgage" or "doctor home loan" have extremely high purchase intent
- Search campaigns convert at higher rates than Performance Max when competing for the same terms
- The niche is specific enough that broad automated targeting (PMax) risks wasting spend on irrelevant audiences
- Performance Max can supplement as a secondary campaign to capture Display/YouTube/Discovery inventory once Search is optimized

**Recommended campaign structure:**
1. **Campaign 1 (Search - High Intent):** Core physician mortgage terms
2. **Campaign 2 (Search - Profession-Specific):** Segmented by profession (dentist, vet, etc.)
3. **Campaign 3 (Performance Max):** Audience signal-driven, using site visitor lists + custom segments, at 20-30% of total budget

These profession-specific terms typically have lower competition (many lenders only bid on "physician") while capturing equally qualified prospects.

**Negative Keywords to Add Immediately:**
- `physician mortgage rates today` (rate shoppers, not local buyers)
- `physician mortgage calculator` (informational, not transactional)
- `physician mortgage refinance` (unless you offer refi)
- `physician mortgage reddit` (research phase)
- `best physician mortgage lender` (comparison shopping)
- `physician mortgage requirements` (early research)

#### Seasonality Insight

Search volume for "physician mortgage" peaks in **April-June** (residency match + graduation season) at 6,600-8,100/mo, and drops to 3,600/mo in winter. **Increase budget 40% from April through June** to capture this spike.

#### Bidding Strategy

- **Start with Manual CPC** for the first 2-4 weeks to establish baseline data (50+ conversions needed)
- **Switch to Target CPA** once you have 30+ conversions in a 30-day window
- **Set initial Target CPA at $80-120** based on mortgage lead benchmarks
- **Use bid adjustments:** +20% for mobile (physicians search on phones during breaks), +15% for 7am-9am and 6pm-10pm (before/after clinical hours)

**Callout Extensions:**

#### Landing Page Optimization

Based on mortgage landing page best practices:
- **Keep the form above the fold** with 4-5 fields max (Name, Email, Phone, Profession, Approximate Purchase Price)
- **Include a rate calculator or pre-qualification widget** to boost engagement
- **Feature trust signals:** NMLS numbers, lender logos, testimonials from medical professionals
- **Compliance:** Include required TILA disclosures, APR disclaimers, "not a commitment to lend" language
- **Target conversion rate:** 3-5% from Google Ads traffic (mortgage industry benchmark)

### 1.2 Meta Ads Strategy

#### Critical Compliance: Double Special Ad Category

A physician mortgage landing page triggers **two** Special Ad Category classifications on Meta:
1. **Housing** - because it relates to real estate/home buying
2. **Credit** - because it promotes a loan/mortgage product (expanded in January 2025 to cover all financial products)

You MUST select both categories. Attempting to avoid this triggers Meta's evasion classifier and risks account suspension.

#### Targeting Restrictions Under Special Ad Category

- **Age:** Fixed to 18-65+ (cannot narrow)
- **Gender:** Must include all genders
- **Location:** Minimum 15-mile radius; cannot target by ZIP code
- **No Lookalike Audiences** available
- **Limited interest targeting** (many demographic options removed)
- **No exclusions** on locations, interests, or demographics

#### Recommended Campaign Structure

**Campaign 1: Cold Prospecting (Awareness + Lead Gen)**

- **Objective:** Lead Generation (using Meta Instant Forms)
- **Targeting approach:** Since you cannot target by income, education, or precise demographics, use **creative as your filter**. The ad content itself qualifies the audience:
  - Interest targeting: "Medical school", "Residency (medicine)", "American Medical Association", "Physician", "Dental school"
  - Geographic targeting: 15-mile radius around Palm Springs / Coachella Valley area
  - Job title targeting (still available): "Physician", "Surgeon", "Dentist", "Veterinarian", "Pharmacist" (limited but functional)
- **Budget:** $15-25/day
- **Placements:** Facebook Feed + Instagram Feed + Instagram Stories (disable Audience Network for lead quality)

**Campaign 2: Retargeting (Warm Audiences)**

- **Objective:** Lead Generation or Conversions (to landing page)
- **Custom Audiences:**
  - Website visitors (landing page) - 30-day window
  - Video viewers (50%+ watch time) - 60-day window
  - Lead form openers who did not submit - 14-day window
  - Instagram/Facebook page engagers - 90-day window
- **Budget:** $10-15/day
- **Retargeting sequence:**
  - Days 1-3: "Still exploring physician mortgage options? Get pre-qualified in 2 minutes"
  - Days 4-14: Testimonial/case study creative
  - Days 15-30: Market urgency or rate-related messaging

#### Creative Format Recommendations

**Best performing formats for real estate lead gen on Meta (2025-2026 data):**

| Format | Avg CTR | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| Lead Form Ads | 2.1% | Highest conversion, lowest friction |
| Video Ads (15-30 sec) | 1.9% | Cold prospecting, education |
| Carousel Ads | 1.7% | Multiple benefits/steps |
| Single Image | 1.4% | Retargeting with simple CTA |

**Recommended creative mix:**
1. **Video (primary cold prospecting):** 15-30 second video explaining "What is a physician mortgage?" with text overlay highlighting 0% down, no PMI. Use a talking head or animated explainer. This builds the warm audience for retargeting.
2. **Lead Form (primary conversion):** Use Meta Instant Forms with "Higher Intent" setting (adds a review step before submission). Pre-fill name and email. Include custom questions: "What is your medical specialty?" and "When do you plan to purchase?"
3. **Carousel (secondary):** 4 cards showing: (1) 0% Down Payment, (2) No PMI, (3) High DTI Allowed, (4) Student Loan Friendly + CTA card

**Creative refresh cadence:** Every 2-3 weeks to prevent fatigue. Cap frequency at 5-7 impressions per user.

#### Lead Form vs. Landing Page

- **Lead Forms:** 3x higher conversion rate, lower CPL (~$15-30), but lower lead quality
- **Landing Page:** Lower conversion rate, higher CPL (~$40-80), but higher quality leads who read the page content
- **Recommendation:** Use Lead Forms for volume, Landing Page conversions for quality. Run both simultaneously and compare lead-to-appointment rate.

### 1.3 Case Studies & Benchmarks

#### Physician Mortgage Market Context

- Medical professionals represent a small but high-value segment with strong credit performance and long-term client value
- Lenders use physician loans as loss leaders to lock in early-career doctors, meaning competition for these leads is fierce at the national level
- Non-bank lenders (Newrez, etc.) are expanding into the physician mortgage broker channel, increasing competition
- The typical physician mortgage borrower: residency or early-career, $150K+ income trajectory, $200K+ student debt, limited liquid savings, credit score 700+

#### Case Study: Physician Mortgage Conversion

A cardiology resident and spouse (combined income $150K, $20K cash reserves) purchased a $600K townhouse with:
- 100% financing (0% down)
- $20K lender credit toward closing costs based on zip code
- Future income used for eligibility determination

This case study format makes excellent ad creative: real scenario, real numbers, relatable situation.

#### Key Competitive Insight

The physician mortgage space is dominated by **content marketing** (Student Loan Planner, White Coat Investor, WealthKeel) rather than paid ads. Most traffic to physician mortgage pages comes from organic search and referral. This means:
1. Paid ads face less competition than you might expect from CPCs alone (most bidders are national lenders, not local agents)
2. A local real estate agent promoting physician mortgages in a specific market (Coachella Valley) occupies a unique niche that national content sites cannot compete with
3. The "local physician mortgage + real estate agent" angle is a significant differentiator

## Page Type 2: Neighborhood/Subdivision Pages

### 2.1 Google Ads Strategy

#### Campaign Type Recommendation: Search (Primary) + Local Services Ads (Secondary)

**Search campaigns** remain the best performer for neighborhood-specific real estate because:
- Users searching "[neighborhood] homes for sale" have clear transactional intent
- Coachella Valley neighborhood CPCs are remarkably low ($0.67-$1.82 top-of-page bids)
- These keywords have strong, consistent search volume

**Local Services Ads** provide a secondary channel:
- CPL of $24-75 for real estate (vs. $100+ for traditional Search)
- Appear above standard Search ads
- Google-verified badge builds trust
- However, LSA quality has declined since Google dropped the "Guaranteed" branding (Oct 2025) and no longer credits junk leads

**Performance Max** is not recommended as the primary campaign for neighborhood pages because the keywords are specific and the geo is narrow -- Search captures this intent much more efficiently.

### 2.2 Meta Ads Strategy

#### Special Ad Category: Housing

All neighborhood real estate ads on Meta MUST use the Housing Special Ad Category. This is non-negotiable. Meta's classifiers now scan ad images for real estate imagery (floor plans, building exteriors, "For Sale" signs) and will auto-flag even if you forget to select it.

#### Campaign Structure

**Campaign 1: Cold Prospecting -- Neighborhood Awareness**

- **Objective:** Lead Generation
- **Targeting:**
  - Geographic: 15-mile radius around each Coachella Valley city (this is the minimum Meta allows under Housing)
  - Interests: "Real estate", "Home buying", "Moving", "Zillow", "Realtor.com" (still available under Housing SAC)
  - Broader interests as filters: "Golf", "Tennis", "Retirement planning" (to attract the Coachella Valley buyer profile)
- **Key insight:** Because demographic targeting is severely limited, your creative must do the filtering. A carousel of luxury Indian Wells homes self-selects a different audience than starter homes in Indio.

**Campaign 2: Retargeting -- Website Visitors**

- **Objective:** Conversions (to lead form on site)
- **Custom Audiences:**
  - Visitors to specific neighborhood pages (30-day window)
  - Visitors who viewed 3+ listing pages (14-day window)
  - Lead form starters who did not complete (7-day window)
- **Sequence:**
  - Days 1-3: "Still looking in [Neighborhood]? 12 new listings this week"
  - Days 4-14: "Homes in [Neighborhood] are selling in [X] days. See what's available"
  - Days 15-30: Market stats CMA-style content, agent intro

**Campaign 3: Lookalike Alternative -- Broad + Creative Filter**

Since Lookalike Audiences are not available under Housing SAC:
- Use Broad targeting (18-65+, 15mi radius) with highly specific creative
- Meta's algorithm will optimize delivery based on who engages
- This effectively creates a "machine learning lookalike" without using the actual Lookalike feature
- Requires $20+/day to give the algorithm enough data

#### Creative Format Recommendations (with 2025-2026 Data)

| Format | CTR | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Carousel (Multi-listing) | 1.7% | Showcasing 4-5 homes in a neighborhood, 3x more clicks than single-property |
| Video (Neighborhood tour) | 1.9% | Cold prospecting, building area awareness |
| Lead Form (Instant) | 2.1% | Highest conversion, "Get new [Neighborhood] listings" |
| Single Image | 1.4% | Retargeting with simple "Schedule a showing" CTA |

**Recommended creative by campaign:**

1. **Cold prospecting:** 60-second neighborhood video tours (drone footage of PGA West, walking tour of El Paseo, etc.) with text overlay of median price + key stats. Real Estate achieves the highest CTRs on Meta at 2.60% using visual-first content.

2. **Listing showcase:** Carousel with 4-5 best listings in a subdivision. Each card: hero photo, price, beds/baths, one standout feature. Final card: "See all [X] listings in [Neighborhood]" with CTA. Case study showed this format drives 3x more clicks than single-property ads.

3. **Market data hook:** Single image or short video with CMA stats overlay: "Indian Wells Median Home Price: $1.2M | Average Days on Market: 42 | See All Active Listings." This leverages the CMA data you already have on 1,424 subdivisions.

4. **Lead magnet:** "Get new [Neighborhood] listings before they hit Zillow" -- Instant Form with Higher Intent setting. Ask for email, budget range, and timeline.

#### Retargeting Best Practices (2025-2026 Data)

- **Optimal retargeting windows:**
  - Website visitors: 30-day window (real estate sales cycle is long)
  - High-intent visitors (3+ pages): 14-day window (these are hot)
  - Form abandoners: 7-day window (urgency)
  - Video viewers (50%+): 60-day window (awareness to consideration)
- **Frequency cap:** 5-7 impressions per user maximum
- **Retargeting lifts conversions up to 40%** when done correctly
- **Refresh creative every 2-3 weeks** to avoid fatigue
- **Budget split:** 70% prospecting, 30% retargeting is the standard starting point; shift to 60/40 as pixel data matures

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Google Ads Mistakes

1. **Bidding on broad match without negatives.** "Physician mortgage" in broad match will trigger ads for "physician assistant salary" or "mortgage calculator." Use phrase match or exact match initially, and build negative keyword lists aggressively.

2. **Sending all traffic to the homepage.** Each ad group should point to its specific neighborhood page or the physician loan landing page. Never send paid traffic to a generic page.

3. **Ignoring ad scheduling.** Physician searches peak before 9am and after 6pm (outside clinical hours). Neighborhood searches peak on weekends. Adjust bids accordingly.

4. **Not using call extensions.** Real estate is high-consideration -- many leads prefer to call. Add Joseph's phone number as a call extension with call tracking.

5. **Neglecting Quality Score.** At $10-13 CPC for physician keywords, every point of Quality Score improvement saves real money. Ensure keyword-ad-landing page alignment is tight.

6. **Running PMax without audience signals.** Performance Max without Custom Segments or customer lists will waste budget on irrelevant placements. Always provide strong audience signals.

### Meta Ads Mistakes

1. **Not selecting Housing + Credit Special Ad Categories.** For the physician mortgage page, BOTH must be selected. For neighborhood pages, Housing must be selected. Meta's classifiers will auto-detect and flag your ad anyway -- failing to self-declare triggers an "Evasion" penalty on account health.

2. **Targeting by ZIP code.** Not possible under Housing SAC. The minimum geo-targeting is a 15-mile radius around a city.

3. **Using Lookalike Audiences.** Not available under any Special Ad Category. Use broad targeting + strong creative instead.

4. **Running too many ad sets with insufficient budget.** Each ad set needs $20+/day to exit the learning phase. Two well-funded ad sets beat five starved ones.

5. **Not using Higher Intent on Lead Forms.** Default Instant Forms auto-submit easily, generating low-quality leads. Enable the "Higher Intent" option which adds a review/confirmation step.

6. **Ignoring the CPM trend.** Meta CPMs rose 70% YoY (Jan 2025 to Jan 2026). Budget more per impression than last year. Offset by improving CTR through better creative.

7. **Forgetting to exclude existing leads.** Upload your CRM contact list as a Custom Audience and exclude them from prospecting campaigns to avoid paying to reach people you already have.

### Cross-Platform Mistakes

1. **Not connecting the CRM.** Every lead from both platforms must flow into the CRM with source attribution. Without this, you cannot measure true ROI.

2. **Slow lead follow-up.** Mortgage and real estate leads decay rapidly -- leads contacted within 5 minutes convert at 8x the rate of those contacted after 30 minutes. Ensure form submissions trigger immediate notifications.

3. **No cross-platform attribution.** Users may see a Meta ad, then search on Google later. Use UTM parameters consistently and review assisted conversions in GA4 to understand the full path.
